The problem

SV2 enables miner autonomy. That autonomy needs guardrails.

⚠

The SV2 adoption challenge

Stratum V2 lets miners propose their own block templates. Pools gain encrypted transport and reduced bandwidth, but lose visibility into what miners submit. Without verification, a pool has no way to detect malformed templates, fee policy violations, or stale prevhash references before distributing work to the network.

✓

The ReserveGrid solution

ReserveGrid OS sits between miners and the pool as a standard job server. It verifies every template against operator policy, switches prevhash references with fail-closed safety, tracks shares through a two-event lifecycle, and emits structured reason codes for every decision. The pool gets SV2 benefits without blind trust.

Share pipeline

Two-event share lifecycle

Every share produces exactly two events, joined by share_id and event_id.

1
Share arrives
Miner submits SubmitSharesStandard over SV2 binary channel
2
Validation
5 checks: channel, job_id, difficulty, ntime, nonce/version_bits
3
share_accepted
ACK sent to miner immediately. Event includes PoW identity and reason_code
4
Upstream POST
Valid share forwarded to pool backend via HTTP with HMAC-SHA256 signing
5
share_forward_result
Forward outcome recorded: forwarded, upstream_accepted, upstream_http_status
1:1 join invariant: Every share_accepted with sv2_response = "success" produces exactly one share_forward_result. The adapter blocks on channel capacity rather than dropping results.
Prevhash safety

Fail-closed prevhash switching

When a new block arrives, the gateway waits for verification before distributing work.

1
New block detected
Template with new prevhash arrives from upstream
2
Verdict requested
TemplatePropose sent to verifier over TCP
3
50ms window
Gateway waits for verdict. Miners stay on last verified job
4a
Verdict accepted
SetNewPrevHash + new job broadcast to all channels
Happy path: Verdict arrives within 50ms. New prevhash and job distributed immediately. Target: p50 < 50ms, p95 < 150ms, p99 < 300ms.
Timeout path: No verdict within 50ms. Miners hold on stale job for up to 5s. If no verdict after 5s, deterministic disconnect with prevhash_switch_timeout reason code.
